## What I think about

- **AI adoption in agriculture** — How farmers, extension systems, and state agencies actually use AI tools under constraints of language, trust, and uneven market access.
- **Agricultural finance and credit intermediation** — Whether digital records and AI-assisted underwriting can meaningfully change access to credit in rural markets, and under what conditions.
- **Technology adoption under institutional frictions** — Why promising technologies often stall between the pilot and the programme, and what that means for how we design and govern public AI.
- **Policy design for AI in low-resource settings** — What governance, evaluation, and public investment structures best support equitable deployment of AI in agriculture, finance, and rural livelihoods.

## Projects

**AgriLM — a domain model for Indian agriculture**
Fine-tuned an open Gemma model into a multilingual agricultural assistant, building the full data pipeline, training workflow, and evaluation stack in Python. An attempt to show how frontier-model methods can be adapted for real public-service contexts — where the language is local, the data is sparse, and the users are farmers, not developers.

**FarmerPay — agri-fintech credit platform**
Designed and built a platform that turns farmer and lending data into credit-decisioning and advisory tools. A working testbed for whether AI and data systems can actually widen access to financial services in rural markets — not as a proof of concept, but as a thing that runs.

**PMFBY crop insurance survey — Uttarakhand**
Built interactive dashboards from primary field surveys covering ~1,862 farmers and 300+ bankers, insurers, and officials. Examines the frictions in agricultural insurance markets and the institutions that share risk for smallholders — where the data was hard-won, not downloaded.
